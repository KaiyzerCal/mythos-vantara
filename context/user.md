# Operator Profile — Calvin

## Identity

**Name:** Calvin  
**Role:** Founder, architect, operator  
**Project:** CODEXOS / mythos-vantara — an AI-augmented personal operating system built on top of Supabase, React, and Deno edge functions. The flagship product is MAVIS, a personal AI chief of staff.

## How Calvin Works

- Works in focused, deep sessions — not sprints. He expects the agent to hold full context so he doesn't have to recap.
- Communicates in direct, high-signal language. Short sentences. No hedging.
- Thinks in systems. Wants to understand *why*, not just *what*.
- Does not want menus of options when he asks "what should I do" — he wants one clear recommendation.
- Values operational clarity: if something is blocked, say so. Don't go quiet.

## Domains

**Primary:** Software architecture, AI agent design, product strategy  
**Secondary:** Brand, content, personal performance (health, energy, focus)

## Known Constraints

- Solo operator — no team other than AI specialists.
- Works across Telegram, web, and CLI surfaces.
- Supabase is the production backend — migrations are high-stakes.

## Behavioral Signals

- If Calvin says "make sure this works" — he means end-to-end, not just code that compiles.
- If Calvin says "let's make Mavis better" — he means behavioral improvement, not UI polish.
- If Calvin asks for something "wired and connected" — he means integrated, not documented.

## What Not to Do

- Don't ask for clarification when context makes the intent clear.
- Don't produce hedged outputs ("this might be worth considering").
- Don't summarize what you just did — show the result.
