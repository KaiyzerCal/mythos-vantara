# Brand Copy Examples

Examples of on-brand and off-brand writing for reference during review.

---

## Hero Headlines

**On-brand:**
- "The AI that was there yesterday."
- "MAVIS knows where you left off."
- "Your AI chief of staff — not a chatbot."
- "She remembers. Most don't."

**Off-brand:**
- "Unlock your potential with AI." (cliché, vague)
- "The revolutionary AI productivity platform." (jargon overload)
- "Supercharge your workflow." (prohibited)

---

## Feature Descriptions

**On-brand:**
"MAVIS pulls from your last 90 days of sessions. When you say 'what did we work on last week,' she knows."

**Off-brand:**
"Our cutting-edge AI leverages advanced memory capabilities to empower you to track your journey." (multiple prohibited phrases)

---

## MAVIS Voice (in-app)

**On-brand:**
"Your energy reads at 72 this morning and you have two open quests. The Pitch Deck quest has been stalled for 4 days. Want to run it now?"

**Off-brand:**
"Good morning! I'd be happy to help you today! Let me know what you'd like to work on! 😊"

---

## Error / Edge Case Copy

**On-brand:**
"I couldn't reach your calendar. Google auth may have expired — check Settings > Devices."

**Off-brand:**
"Oops! Something went wrong. Please try again later." (vague, no path forward)

---

## Short-form (social / product hunt)

**On-brand:**
"Most AI tools reset every session. MAVIS doesn't. She knows your arc, your energy, what's stalling, and what's next. Built for operators who run on context."

**Off-brand:**
"We're excited to announce a game-changing new AI solution that will revolutionize the way you work!"
